Step 2

Propose

Reading procurement criteria to win contracts

Once needs are understood, the next step is designing a persuasive proposal. I trained this skill repeatedly by analyzing government and public institution bid announcements and writing proposals.

Each agency had different evaluation criteria. Agencies focused on expanding channels for small businesses cared more about the number of participating businesses and regional impact than revenue, while those tied to donations required public value to be presented in concrete numbers. I designed different business concepts, execution plans, and performance metrics tailored to each agency's criteria.

More important than good writing was first understanding what basis the client agency used to make its decision.

Step 3

Persuade

Proving it directly in front of the customer

No matter how thorough the perception and proposal, if you can't persuade the customer face to face, the deal doesn't happen.

When planning the live commerce business, I stepped in as show host to get closer to customers. Instead of listing product features, I explained why customers should choose each product from a buyer's perspective, and immediately adjusted my emphasis and approach as I read real-time reactions. I repeatedly experienced the moment when customer understanding translated into a purchase decision.

I leveraged generative AI to elevate the quality of persuasion. I used AI to write broadcast scripts tailored to each day's products, and produced recipe videos and opening videos to help customers understand what they were buying. This enhanced approach to persuasion earned me the Best Prize at the company's AI utilization competition. As production processes became more efficient, broadcast volume grew — ultimately securing ₩627M in SME live commerce support project funding.

Persuasion also comes from language. IELTS Band 7 and 70+ hours of simultaneous interpretation at US/Germany-based conferences were not simply foreign language training. Understanding the other person's language and instantly restructuring it in their context — it was communication itself that was being trained. Interpretation is not only needed between the languages of different countries. In the sales arena too, moving between the customer's expectations and the seller's language is, I believe, the essence of persuasion. With overseas clients, I can engage immediately without language barriers and persuade on the spot.
